The V15 Detect combines strong suction, intelligent sensors, and practical cleaning tools into a balanced package. The Fluffy Optic cleaner head uses a green laser angled at 1.5 degrees — it highlights fine dust particles that are normally invisible under regular lighting. Users tend to clean more thoroughly because they can see the dust clearly.

The Piezo sensor uses acoustic vibrations to detect dust particles, processing data thousands of times per second. When it detects more debris, the motor automatically increases suction. The graph on the LCD isn't just for display — it directly influences how the vacuum adjusts in real time. Anti-tangle vanes guide hair directly into the bin instead of wrapping around the brush. In Auto mode, battery drains more efficiently — around 40–45 minutes of practical runtime.

The most powerful cordless vacuum in Dyson's lineup. In real use, 280–300 AW translates to deeper carpet cleaning and better pickup of embedded debris. The built-in dusting and crevice tool hidden inside the wand is one of the most practical upgrades — instead of stopping to switch attachments, you simply detach the wand and start using the tool immediately.

The shift to a single-button power system reduces finger fatigue during longer sessions — you no longer need to hold down a trigger constantly. HEPA filtration captures 99.9% of particles as small as 0.1 microns. At ~8.6 lbs, it's noticeably heavier — over short sessions manageable, but during longer above-shoulder cleaning, wrist fatigue becomes a factor.

At just 5.22 lbs, this is the lightest "Detect" model in the lineup. The difference is felt immediately — moving between rooms, cleaning stairs, or reaching high corners becomes much easier. For people with wrist pain or joint sensitivity, this is often the deciding factor. The button-start system (no trigger holding) further reduces comfort during longer sessions.

Dustbin at 0.35L means emptying more frequently — in a small apartment, manageable; in larger spaces, more interruptions.

The V8 still works well for lighter cleaning and remains a practical option for budget-conscious buyers. On hard floors and low-pile carpets it handles dust and small debris without trouble. Newer versions include the Hair Screw Tool previously limited to more expensive models — works well for removing hair from upholstery, car seats, and smaller surfaces.

Which Dyson Should You Buy?

If you want a balanced option that handles most situations well, the Dyson V15 Detect is the most reliable choice. It combines strong suction, smart features, and practical usability without forcing compromise. If power is your priority, the Gen5detect delivers the highest suction but comes with added weight. If comfort matters more, the V12 Detect Slim offers lightweight handling for smaller spaces. The V8 remains solid for budget-conscious users, and the V15s Submarine adds wet cleaning for those who want an all-in-one approach.
