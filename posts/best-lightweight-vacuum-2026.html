Common Buying Mistakes to Avoid

Ignoring real-world battery life: Battery claims often reflect Eco mode with no attachments. Always look at Auto or normal mode runtime — that's how you'll actually use it. Choosing based on weight alone: Balance, wheel design, and floor movement matter more. Some heavier models feel easier because they glide smoothly. Overlooking bin size: A small bin in a large home means constant interruptions. Buying features you won't use: If you don't need data tracking or self-emptying, a simpler model may serve you better at lower cost.
